Sri or Lakshmi, is depicted in the Vedas as the goddess of wealth and fortune, power and beauty. According to the Puranas, she was the daughter of sage Bhrigu and his wife Khyati. She was later born from the Kshir Sagar (the ocean of milk), while the more popular churning was known as Samudra Manthan. Hence, his appeal, "Ksheer sea king Tanay".
