Shardiya Durga Navratri Yagya

Shardiya Durga Navratri Yagya special (festival) is celebrated as Vedic Yagya. The word Navratri means 9 nights, in which nine forms of Yagya (worship) of Durga Devi are performed. The tenth day is celebrated as Dussehra. According to the Hindu month, this Yagya is performed for 9 days from Pratipada to Navami Tithi. In this Yagya, nine forms of three goddesses Mahalakshmi, Mahasaraswati, and Mahakali are worshipped. 

Navratri Yagya is very important and prominent in the Vedic tradition. Navratri Yagya is performed with reverence, devotion, and emotion. The concept of Maa Durga is the most auspicious and unique period for devotion and the power of the divine. Overcoming the evil instincts of ego, anger, lust and other animal instincts is a void experience. This void is full of spiritual wealth. For this purpose, one gets all the materialistic, spiritual wealth and prosperity.
